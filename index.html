About Me

Hello! I’m Binghan Liu, a recent PhD graduate in astrophysics from the University of Leeds. My research focuses on simulating the climates and potential habitability of Earth-like exoplanets, especially those in eccentric or tidally-locked orbits. I grew up fascinated by astronomy, and over the years, my passion has led me from stargazing in my hometown to working with global climate models like WACCM6. I’m now exploring how to apply my data and modeling skills to new challenges, including in data science and education.

Research Projects

  • Higher water loss on eccentric Earth-like exoplanets — Published in MNRAS (2023). Found that eccentricity increases surface temperature and atmospheric water loss due to reduced albedo and cloud effects.
  • Habitability enhancement from eccentric orbits — Published in MNRAS (2024). Showed that seasonal heating in eccentric orbits may expand habitable zones to higher latitudes.
  • Climate of tidally locked Proxima Centauri b — Research completed. Found the planet sustains an eyeball ocean with potential for habitability around the substellar point.

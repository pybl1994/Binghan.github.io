Highlighted Research Projects

Higher Water Loss on Earth-like Exoplanets in Eccentric Orbits

Published in MNRAS (Sep 2023): Demonstrated that orbital eccentricity can significantly raise global temperatures and increase water vapor escape from the atmosphere, potentially altering the long-term habitability of planets.

Eccentric Orbits May Enhance the Habitability of Earth-like Exoplanets

Published in MNRAS (Aug 2024): Found that moderate eccentricity can extend habitable zones by distributing heat and reducing ice cover, with implications for planetary habitability and observation strategies.

Climate Simulation of Proxima Centauri b

Ongoing: Simulating a tidally locked ocean world with reduced ozone due to weak UV flux. Despite the cold average temperature, the planet supports a liquid water region at the substellar point.

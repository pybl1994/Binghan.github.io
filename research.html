Exoplanetary Research

3D Earth‐System Modelling & Observability of Alien Climates

As a PhD Researcher at the University of Leeds (2020–2025), I was involved in the adaptation of the CESM2‐WACCM6 3D Earth‐system model for exoplanet climates—one of the first fully coupled simulations of eccentric orbits in planetary science.

  • Designed and ran over 100 controlled simulation experiments on HPC clusters, managing more than 1 PB of structured climate data.
  • Applied statistical and physical modelling to quantify how orbital eccentricity and tidal locking impact heat redistribution, surface temperature stability, and water-loss rates.
  • Published two first-author papers in MNRAS, including findings that moderate eccentricity can make Earth-like worlds “super-habitable.”
  • Developed Python-based workflows using NASA’s Planetary Spectrum Generator to simulate transmission and emission spectra, identifying biosignature detection opportunities for JWST and future telescopes.
